Kathy Meng is a NYC-based filmmaker and a graduate of NYU’s Tisch School of the Arts. She draws inspiration from her upbringing in Beijing and Massachusetts to tell multicultural stories. Her films are rooted in realism, depicting modern Chinese women who long for intimacy that they can’t find as outsiders in American society. She is a finalist for the Richie Jackson Artist Fellowship, Marcie Bloom Fellowship, and Jacob Burns Fellowship.

 

Besides filmmaking, she enjoys long walks on the beach, hiking, architecture & design, swimming, thrifting, meme-hunting, theater, dancing, reading, tennis, Pictionary, and more.
